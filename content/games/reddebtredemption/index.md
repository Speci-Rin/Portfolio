### Overview

Red Debt Redemption is a shooting game where the player must make choices and build skills to overcome their debt, which appear in the form of various monsters. The player makes decisions about their in-game life choices, which in turn effect the amount of debt they must pay off later.

Submission for SheInnovates Hackathon 2026, which took place over the course of 2 days. Executed menu screen, level selection, and writing. Group worked in Godot, a new platform for every group member. The game is a top down shooter with the goal of teaching college students how to manage their debts.

### Problem

Our main problem was that Godot was a new system for all of us, and we sought out to create a working game to become familiar with the application. We approached this by having the gameplay and scenes do familiar actions such as movement and scene switching. By relating Godot to systems we already knew such as Unity, we were better able to grasp how it works in its similarities and differences.

### Role

My role included collaborating on the design of the main premise, organizing workflow, and implimenting interactive decision making. The design and organization included creating and narrowing down ideas, as well as sketching possible story boards and interactions on a whiteboard. The implimentation included scene switching, dialogue writing, and coding in GDScript.

### Reflection

Overall, this experience led be to be more confident in Godot, and introduced me to GDScript. I feel that I will use this application in the future for smaller games, and it is a great tool alongside Unity. The hackathon enviornment allowed me to try to impliment an experience through Godot without committing to a long term project. I was also excited to develop my skills in working with a team in a time crunch.
